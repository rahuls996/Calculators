# ACKO UX Writing Skill

Write clear, concise, user-centered interface copy for ACKO's digital insurance platform. This skill provides frameworks, patterns, and best practices for creating text that helps Indian consumers understand, purchase, and claim insurance with confidence — across car, bike, health, term life, and travel products.

**Sources**: ACKO Copy Playbook v1.0 + CX Communication Ebook (Conversations with a Customer) + existing UX writing guidelines.

Use this skill when:
- Writing interface copy for ACKO's app or website (buttons, labels, titles, messages, forms)
- Editing existing ACKO UX text for clarity and effectiveness
- Creating error messages, notifications, or success messages for insurance flows
- Designing onboarding, quote generation, policy purchase, or claim journeys
- Establishing or auditing ACKO's voice and tone consistency

## ACKO Brand Context

### About ACKO
ACKO is India's first fully digital insurance company (est. 2016, IRDAI Reg. No. 157). All operations — from purchase to claims — happen through the app and website. No agents, no paperwork, no branch visits.

### Products
- **Car Insurance**: Comprehensive, third-party, own-damage, zero-dep, engine protect
- **Bike Insurance**: Two-wheeler comprehensive and third-party
- **Health Insurance**: Individual and family floater plans, zero waiting period options

- **Travel Insurance**: Domestic and international, visa-compliant

### Target Audience
- Tech-savvy millennials and Gen Z (25–40 primary)

- Digitally comfortable but often insurance-illiterate
- Price-conscious but value transparency and speed

### Brand Tagline
**"Welcome Change"** — ACKO positions itself as the refreshing alternative to traditional, opaque, paperwork-heavy insurance.

## ACKO Voice

ACKO's voice is a constant — the personality that shows up in every interaction regardless of channel or context.

### The 4Bs of Brand Voice

#### 1. Be Relatable
Use real-life situations and examples that resonate with users and create an instant connection.

#### 2. Bring Out the Pain Points
The user is the hero of the story. Focus on their problems and position ACKO as the solution.

#### 3. Build Trust
Use clear and straightforward messages while avoiding insurance jargon.

#### 4. Be Forward-Thinking
Position ACKO as a modern and user-centric insurance provider.

> **Avoid clichés**: Never use words like "redefining", "revolutionising", "seamless", "game-changing", "cutting-edge".

### Voice Spectrum (Always stay within these bounds)

| Quality | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Informal but not too casual | "You have to see this, it's really good!" | "Yo, you gotta check out this crazy offer." |
| Detailed but jargon-free | "We cover it all, from cotton balls to syringes." | "We cover all consumables in your bill." |
| Straightforward but not boring | "A plan so good, it pays 100% of your bill." | "We value you so we will pay 100% of your bill." |
| Friendly but not pleasing | "We are always here if you have questions." | "We are here for you because we value you." |

### User-Forward vs. ACKO-Forward

Always write **user-forward**, not ACKO-forward. WhatsApp, emails, and notifications are crowded — users only pay attention to what gives them value.

**ACKO-forward (avoid):**
- This is what we are
- This is what we have done
- This is why we are cool

**User-forward (aim for):**
- This is what we know about you
- This is what you are facing
- This is how we can solve this
- This is why we are right for you

## Tone (Adaptive to Context)

While voice stays consistent, tone shifts based on the user's situation.

### In-App / Product Contexts

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Buying / Exploring | Light, encouraging, benefit-focused | "Car insurance from ₹2,762/year. Zero paperwork. Seriously." |
| Comparing / Deciding | Clear, factual, comparison-friendly | "Comprehensive covers your car + others. Third-party covers only others." |
| Claiming | Empathetic, calm, step-by-step | "We know this is stressful. Let's get your claim sorted." |
| Renewing | Quick, efficient, value-reinforcing | "Your policy expires 15 Mar. Renew in 60 seconds and keep your 50% NCB." |
| Onboarding | Patient, welcoming, educational | "Welcome to ACKO. Let's find the right cover. Takes about 2 minutes." |
| Error / Failure | Empathetic, solution-first, no blame | "Payment didn't go through. Your card wasn't charged. Try again or use UPI." |
| Success / Confirmation | Positive, brief, next-step oriented | "You're covered! Policy sent to your email." |
| Regulatory / Legal | Clear, structured, plain-language | "In simple terms: if you don't renew within 90 days, you'll lose your NCB." |

### CX / Communication Contexts

| Situation | Tone to Use |
|---|---|
| User is filing a claim | Focused empathy |
| Rejecting a claim | Focused empathy |
| Unable to cover members | Focused empathy |
| Replying to angry comments/tweets | Focused empathy |
| Payment reminders | Helpful |
| User is seeking information | Helpful |
| We want user to complete an action | Helpful |
| Delayed responses | Authoritative |
| Dealing with fraud | Authoritative |
| Replying to false allegations | Authoritative |
| Possible consequences | Authoritative |

> **Always be solution-oriented.** The user is reaching out to complete a task and get a sense of achievement. Enable them to do that as quickly as possible.

## Copy Frameworks

Use these frameworks before drafting any message, screen, or communication.

### Framework 1: 4Cs

Every piece of ACKO copy must be:

| C | Question to ask | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Clear** | Can a layman get it? | No jargon, no ambiguity |
| **Concise** | Can I reduce more words? | Every word earns its place |
| **Compelling** | Does it sound interesting? | Gives the user a reason to care |
| **Credible** | Have I supported it with proof? | Data, facts, social proof |

**Example:**
> "Get India's #1 health plan *(Credible)* to turn your hospital bills to zero *(Clear)* and never pay out of your pocket again *(Compelling)*."

### Framework 2: FAB

Use for product explanations, plan introductions, and sales copy.

| F | Features | Introduce the unique selling point |
|---|---|---|
| A | Advantages | Talk about other advantages |
| B | Benefits | Highlight what's in it for them |

**Example:**
> "See a doctor without visiting the doctor. *(Feature)*
> Get free doctor teleconsultation with ACKO health insurance. *(Benefit)*"

### Framework 3: PAS

Use for marketing copy, WhatsApp campaigns, and re-engagement.

| P | Problem | Identify the user's problem |
|---|---|---|
| A | Agitate | Make the problem feel real and urgent |
| S | Solution | Pitch the product as the answer |

**Example:**
> "Worried about missing your flight? *(Problem)*
> Baggage loss can turn your trip into a bad trip. *(Agitate)*
> Get ACKO International Travel Insurance and claim for missed flights. *(Solution)*"

> **Choosing the right framework:**
> - Use **4Cs** as a quality check on all copy
> - Use **FAB** when introducing a product or feature
> - Use **PAS** in marketing, WhatsApp, or re-engagement flows

## Value Propositions

Before drafting any message, be clear about which value you're adding:

| Type | What it does | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Informational value** | Answers a concern or gives useful context | Education flows, FAQs, tooltips |
| **Service value** | Explains features and what they do for the user | Plan pages, comparison screens |
| **Incentive value** | Highlights discounts, rewards, starting price | Marketing, renewal, re-engagement |

> You can include multiple value propositions in a single message. Just be clear about which one leads.

## Copy Principles

### Story over Specification
You are not selling a product — you are telling a story. The product is the supporting character in the user's story. The goal is not to make them feel something about the product — it is to make them feel something about themselves.

> Highlight the user's problem. Pitch the product as the solution.

### Clarity over Cleverness
Aim to be clear, not clever. Clever can be confusing — especially for first-time insurance buyers.

### The Telegram Trick
Write as if every word costs money. You're paying for each one. Ask: "Do I need *very* in that sentence?" Cut everything that doesn't earn its place.

### The Zombie Test for Active Voice
After every verb, insert "by zombies." If the sentence still makes sense, you're using passive voice — rewrite it.

- "The story was written (by zombies)" → passive ❌
- "She wrote (by zombies) the story" → active ✅

## Copy Tenets (Must-Haves for Every Piece)

These are non-negotiable rules that apply to all ACKO copy — app, website, email, WhatsApp, call scripts.

| Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|---|
| ACKO always uppercase | ACKO app | Acko app |
| British English | Hospitalisation, customisation, enrolment, lakhs | Hospitalization, customization, enrollment, lacs |
| ₹ symbol, not Rs or INR | Plans starting at ₹543/month | Plans starting at INR 543/month |
| Sentence case everywhere | Cover your family now | Cover Your Family Now |
| Title case for proper nouns | ACKO Platinum Health Plan | acko platinum health plan |
| Oxford comma | Name, age, and location | Name, age and location |
| Active voice | We cover all hospitalisation charges | All hospitalisation charges are covered |
| Days of week — full, capitalised | Wednesday | Wed |
| Currency abbreviations with space | ₹5 L coverage, ₹2 Cr coverage | ₹5L coverage, ₹2Cr |
| 12-hour time format | 5 PM | 17:00 |
| Dates in Day/Month/Year | 12th December 2024 | December 12th 2024 |
| Full stop at end of messages | ...update. Thanks for your patience. | ...patience (no full stop) |
| Links at end of sentences | Insure your family at \<link\> | Use this link — \<link\> — to insure |
| 'Policyholder' without a space | Policyholder | Policy holder |
| Capitalise: IRDAI, Zero Depreciation Cover, The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 | IRDAI, Zero Depreciation Cover | Irdai, zero depreciation cover |
| Abbreviations — full form first, each word capitalised | Insured Declared Value (IDV) | IDV is nothing but the market value |
| Don't impose morals/lessons | Riya's mistake cost her a fortune | You should learn from Riya's mistake |
| Sentence case for buttons | Cover your family now | Cover Your Family Now |

## Using 'Please' Correctly

| When to use | Example |
|---|---|
| To soften a message | "We were unable to process your cashless claim. Please submit a reimbursement claim instead." |
| When user is waiting | "Please wait while we look for an agent." |
| When user has to do something extra | "We were unable to approve your claim. Please upload the right documents to move ahead." |

| When NOT to use | Example (wrong) |
|---|---|
| For the sake of it | "Please check your premium details." ❌ |

---

## Using 'Sorry' Correctly

| When to use | Example |
|---|---|
| When it's our fault | "Sorry, we had to cancel your medical tests as the doctor was unavailable." |
| When we don't want to explicitly apologise but still show care | "This usually doesn't take this long." |

**Never follow 'sorry' with 'but':**
> ❌ "Sorry for the delay but we are trying our best to connect you to an available agent."

## Delivering Bad News

> "Delivering bad news plainly is an act of respect. The longer you build up to it, the harder it becomes to accept."

**Do**: Lead with the bad news directly, then provide context and next steps.
> ✅ "We were unable to approve your claim as your documents were outdated."

**Don't**: Build up to it, soften it excessively, or bury it.
> ❌ "We have some bad news. It may be disheartening but your claim is rejected."

## Empathy in ACKO Copy

### Scripted Empathy vs. Focused Empathy

**Scripted empathy** (avoid): Generic, surface-level statements that sound empathetic but feel robotic. Relies on clichés and assumptions. Doesn't address the user's actual situation.

**Focused empathy** (aim for): Carefully crafted statements that directly address the user's specific situation with sincerity and relevance. Combines understanding of emotions with actionable next steps.

### Rules of Focused Empathy

**Rule 1: Acknowledge the situation — don't assume the emotion**

❌ "We understand that dealing with a claim can be stressful, especially after an unfortunate event."
*(Assumes the process is stressful — not always true. Sounds scripted.)*

✅ "We are sorry for the unfortunate event. We are here for you and will need the following documents to process your claim as quickly as possible."
*(Directly acknowledges the event. Provides reassurance through action.)*

---

**Rule 2: Reassure through specifics — not empty phrases**

❌ "Rest assured, we're doing everything we can to expedite your claim. Your peace of mind is our top priority."
*(Three empathy phrases in two lines. Vague. No actual information.)*

✅ "We are sorry about the unfortunate event. We are here for you. Please send the following documents for us to process your claim as quickly as possible."
*(One acknowledgement. One reassurance. One action.)*

---

**Rule 3: Express understanding of delays — give real timelines**

❌ "We know waiting can be difficult, especially for something as important as your claim. We appreciate your patience and are working hard to resolve this."
*(Acknowledges waiting — which only amplifies frustration. No timeline.)*

✅ "Our claim processing usually doesn't take this long. Please give us another 24 hours to get back to you with an update. Thanks for your patience."
*(Recognises it's unusual. Gives a concrete timeline. Shifts focus to action.)*

---

> **Focused empathy in one line**: Acknowledge the event → reassure with what you're doing → give them clear next steps.

## UX Text Patterns for ACKO

### Titles
- **Purpose**: Orient users to where they are
- **Format**: Noun phrases, sentence case. No full stop unless another punctuation mark appears in the heading.
- **Examples**: "Your policies", "Get a quote", "Claim status", "Choose your plan"
- **Rule**: Must make sense on its own — don't depend on subtext to complete meaning

### Subtext / Body Copy
- Use only when necessary — most users don't read it
- Use for secondary info that doesn't belong in the title
- If info is truly important, put it in the title, not the subtext

### Buttons and CTAs

**Format**: Active imperative verbs, sentence case, max 6 words, no punctuation (apostrophes are fine)

**Write CTAs from the user's POV** — it's their response to what you're saying:

| Situation | CTA |
|---|---|
| Beginning of a journey | "Tell me more about the plan" |
| Key touchpoint / recommendation | "See my recommended plan" |
| After an explanation | "Got it" |
| After an error, info, or end screen | "Okay" |
| To keep the journey going | "Continue" |

**Do**: "Start claim", "Renew policy", "Download policy", "Track claim", "Cover your family now"
**Don't**: "Submit", "Proceed", "Click here", "OK" (use "Okay"), "CONTINUE" (title case)

### Error Messages

**Validation Errors (Inline)**
- Pattern: `[Field] [specific requirement]`
- "Enter your 10-digit mobile number"
- "Vehicle registration number must be like KA01AB1234"
- "Date of birth can't be in the future"

**Payment Errors**
- Pattern: `[What failed]. [Reassurance]. [Recovery step].`
- "Payment didn't go through. Your card wasn't charged. Try again or use UPI."
- "UPI request timed out. No money was deducted. Try again."

**Policy Errors**
- Pattern: `[Action failed]. [Why]. [What to do].`
- "Couldn't generate your quote. Check your vehicle number and try again."

**Claim Errors**
- Pattern: `[What happened]. [Reassurance]. [Next step].`
- "Photo upload failed. Your claim is safe. Check your connection and retry."

**Never in error messages**: Technical codes without context, blame language ("Invalid input"), jargon, robotic tone, dead ends with no recovery path, threats.

### Success Messages
- Pattern: `[Action] [result/benefit]`
- "You're covered! Policy details sent to your email."
- "Claim submitted. Track it in the app."
- "Renewal done! NCB discount of 50% applied."

### Empty States
- First-use: "No policies yet. Get a quote in 2 minutes."
- No claims: "No claims filed. That's great — your NCB keeps growing!"
- No results: "No garages found near you. Try a different pincode or call us."

### Form Fields

**Labels**: Clear noun phrases — "Vehicle registration number", "Date of birth", "PAN number"

**Helper text**: Explain why ACKO needs it
- "We need your PAN for tax benefits under Section 80D"
- "Your mobile number is used for OTP verification and policy updates"

**Placeholder text**: Show format expected
- "KA01AB1234", "9876543210", "ABCDE1234F", "DD/MM/YYYY"

**Input questions (conversational)**
- "Where do you live?" → Field: "Enter your pin code"
- "What's your age?" → Field: "Enter your age in years"
- "What's your date of birth?" → Field: "Enter in dd/mm/yyyy"

### Notifications

**Action-Required**:
- Title: "Policy expiring soon"
- Body: "Your car insurance expires on 15 Mar. Renew now to keep your 50% NCB."
- CTA: "Renew now"

**Claim Update**:
- Title: "Claim update"
- Body: "Your car is ready for pickup at AutoFix Garage, Koramangala."
- CTA: "Get directions"

**Passive Info**:
- Title: "Challan alert"
- Body: "You have an unpaid traffic challan of ₹500 on KA01AB1234."
- CTA: "Pay challan"

### Regulatory Disclosures

IRDAI requires certain disclosures. Write them clearly:
- Pattern: Plain-language summary + legal footnote
- "ACKO General Insurance Ltd. | IRDAI Reg. No. 157 | Your premium includes 18% GST."
- Keep mandatory disclosures in a clearly marked footnote — not mixed into the UX flow

## Punctuation Rules

### When to Use a Comma
- To break long sentences
- For listing 3 or more things (Oxford comma required)
> "ACKO has health, life, and travel insurance."

### When to Use a Hyphen
- To separate prefix from a word that creates a double vowel: "re-enter"
- For compound words: "mother-in-law"
- For clarity: "Re-cover" (cover again) vs "Recover" (get better)

### Heading Punctuation
- Never use a full stop to end a page title, heading, or subtext
- **Exception**: If any other punctuation (except apostrophe) appears in the heading/subtext, end it with a full stop

### Em Dash Usage
- Use for a strong break or to emphasise a point: "Renew now — keep your 50% NCB."
- Not for ranges (use en dash for ranges: Mon–Wed)

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### In-App Copy (Screens, Flows, Components)

- One message per line — multiple messages on one line confuse users
- Use bold or colour to highlight key information
- Minimise subtext — if it's important, it belongs in the title
- Always keep a CTA in the first fold
- Paragraphs: use full sentences; don't depend on truncation for meaning

### WhatsApp Guidelines

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Don't waste the first line | Never start with "Hey" or pleasantries |
| Bold the first line | It's the hook — grab attention immediately |
| Stay under 100 words | More than 60 words requires scrolling |
| Max 5 emojis per message | Emojis work — but don't abuse them. Makes ACKO look childish. |
| Never emoji mid-sentence | ❌ "Your 🚗 is in safe hands" → ✅ "Your car is in safe hands 🚗" |
| No sign-off needed | WhatsApp is personal — no "Thanks, Team ACKO" |
| Max 3 quick reply buttons | More than 3 causes confusion and drop-off |

**WhatsApp Template Structure:**
1. **Header (up to 2 lines, no emojis)**: The hook
2. **Paragraph 1 (2–3 lines)**: Context / product intro / story
3. **Paragraph 2**: Key message — use bullet points (max 4, only add 4th if necessary)
4. **Section 3**: Limited offer, starting price, CTA link
5. **Buttons (max 3)**: Quick replies

**Marketing WhatsApp Example:**
> So much has happened since you last explored our ACKO Platinum Health Plan.
>
> ✔ You can now add your parents and in-laws to your plan.
> ✔ Our cashless hospital network has grown to 14,300+ hospitals.
> ✔ 9/10 claims are settled in less than 24 hours.
>
> As our car insurance customer, you're eligible for a flat 20% off on health insurance.
>
> Check your discounted price at \<link\>
>
> [Protect my family] [I already have a plan]

### Email Guidelines

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Subject line under 8 words | Longer lines get cut off |
| Subject line should be focused | ✅ "Payment successful for your plan" ❌ "We have good news about your plan" |
| Start heading with 'we' or 'you' | ✅ "You need to visit a lab" ❌ "Lab visit required" |
| CTA in the first fold | Don't make users scroll to take action |
| Don't put names in the heading | Changes meaning, affects layout |
| No sign-off "Team ACKO" | ✅ "Thanks." ❌ "Yours sincerely, Team ACKO." |
| Subtext is optional | Skip if not necessary |

**Informational Email Structure:**
1. Heading (key message, max 2 lines, no full stop)
2. CTA (first fold)
3. Secondary info / pending tasks
4. Footer with support contact

## Guiding Principles by Journey Type

### Pre-Sales
Listen intently, understand what the user needs, then position the product as the best solution. Make the customer the hero — ACKO is the supporting actor.

### Claims
Use focused empathy instead of scripted empathy. The user's emotional state is elevated — acknowledge the event, not the process. Move quickly to what they need to do next.

### Post-Purchase
Users contact ACKO with a specific goal in mind. Help them achieve it quickly. Reduce steps, reduce friction, give them the sense of completion.

## Editing Process

### Phase 1: Purposeful
- Does this help the user buy, renew, or claim?
- Does it reduce anxiety or confusion?
- Is the value to the user immediately clear?
- Does it meet IRDAI disclosure requirements where applicable?

### Phase 2: Concise
- Can any words be removed without losing meaning?
- Is insurance jargon replaced or explained?
- Is the most important information first (price, deadline, action)?
- Are amounts in ₹ with Indian formatting?

### Phase 3: Conversational
- Would you say this to a friend explaining their insurance?
- Is it in active voice?
- Does it avoid "policyholder", "insured", "herein", "aforementioned"?
- Does it sound like ACKO, not like a legacy insurer?

### Phase 4: Clear
- Will a first-time insurance buyer understand this?
- Are terms consistent throughout?
- Is the reading level appropriate (7th–8th grade)?
- Is every action unambiguous?

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Using legacy insurance language ("intimate a claim", "sum assured", "proposer")
- Showing raw policy numbers or codes without context
- Mixing marketing tone into claim or error flows
- Forgetting to explain insurance terms on first use
- Using passive voice in critical moments
- Showing prices without ₹ symbol or without GST clarity
- Being vague about timelines ("shortly" → "within 2 hours")
- Sounding robotic when the user is stressed (claims, payment failures)
- Inconsistent terminology across app, website, and emails
- Skipping mandatory IRDAI disclosures
- Starting WhatsApp messages with "Hey" or generic pleasantries
- Using scripted empathy phrases ("we understand your frustration", "rest assured")
- Following "sorry" with "but"
- Building up to bad news instead of leading with it
- Using clichés like "redefining", "revolutionising", "seamless"
- Writing ACKO-forward instead of user-forward copy
- Asking multiple questions at once in any touchpoint
- Thanking the customer for every minor detail they provide
- Making assumptions about the customer's emotional state
- Using ALL CAPS in CTAs or headings

## Accessibility

### India-Specific Considerations

**Language Accessibility**
- Write in simple English that works for non-native English speakers
- Avoid idioms and cultural references that don't translate
- Plan for text expansion when translating to Hindi, Tamil, Kannada, etc. (+20–40%)

**Digital Literacy**
- Many ACKO users are buying insurance online for the first time
- Never assume familiarity with insurance processes
- Show progress clearly: "Step 2 of 3: Choose your plan"
- Provide clear exits: "Save and continue later"

**Screen Reader Optimization**
- Label all interactive elements explicitly: "Get car insurance quote" not "Get quote"
- Write descriptive link text: "View policy details" not "Click here"
- Error messages should work when read aloud with field names

**Mobile-First**
- 85%+ of ACKO users are on mobile
- Keep text short enough to read without scrolling
- Buttons must be thumb-friendly with clear labels
- Use progressive disclosure for complex insurance details

**Cognitive Accessibility**
- Target 8–14 words per sentence for critical content
- Break complex insurance info into bite-sized chunks
- Use clear headings and logical flow
- One decision per screen where possible

## Quick Reference

**Sentence case**: "Get car insurance" (not "Get Car Insurance")
**Active imperative for buttons**: "Start claim" (not "Claim initiation")
**User-focused**: "Save up to 85% on your premium" (not "We offer competitive pricing")
**Specific verbs**: "Renew" (not "Continue"), "Download" (not "Access")
**Front-loaded**: "₹2,762/year for car insurance" (not "Car insurance available starting from a premium of ₹2,762")
**Indian formatting**: "₹15 lakh" (not "₹15,00,000" or "₹1.5 million")
**Explain jargon**: "Zero dep (we pay full repair cost)" (not just "Zero depreciation add-on")
**No sign-offs in WhatsApp**: Personal, conversational — no "Team ACKO"
**No title case CTAs**: "Cover your family now" not "Cover Your Family Now"
**Empathy through action**: Acknowledge → reassure → next steps (not "Rest assured we value your patience")
